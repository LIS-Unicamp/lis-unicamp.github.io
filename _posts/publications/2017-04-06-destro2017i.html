Similarity measures calculate the degree of relatedness between concepts to support matching between two distinct ontologies. Cross-language matching remains an open research issue due to the difficulties in taking advantage of similarity computation. This article investigates the effects of different semantic similarity measures on the identification of cross-language mappings. We carry out experiments exploring real-world biomedical ontology mappings to comprehend the behaviour of computed similarity values.
